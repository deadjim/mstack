**Interesting signal:** Marketers are already using Claude Code for marketing tasks (MKT1 newsletter documents this). mstack standardizes what they're improvising.

## Current workarounds

- Manual community posting, getting banned from subreddits for self-promotion
- "Build in public" as a substitute for actual marketing strategy
- Paying Jasper/Copy.ai for generic output they rewrite anyway
- Relentless individual outreach — "I was relentless. Every post, every person."
- Writing one polished launch post and hoping it goes viral

## Competitor positioning — what's been said to death

- **Jasper:** "Put AI agents to work for marketing" / "delivering speed, control, and measurable impact"
- **Copy.ai:** "The First AI-Native GTM Platform" / "Infuse AI across your go-to-market engine"
- **CoLaunchly:** "A marketing co-pilot for devs who hate marketing"
- **QuickMarketFit:** "For engineers who hate marketing but need users"
- **Directonaut:** "For Founders who hate marketing"

**What every competitor says:** speed, automation, AI, teams, platform, workflows.
**What nobody says:** research first, hypothesis testing, terminal-native, $0.02/run.

## The gap

Every tool above is a dashboard. Every tool above was built for marketing teams.
None of them live in the terminal. None of them integrate with Claude Code.
None of them treat the first launch as a hypothesis to test, not a campaign to polish.

The gap: **research-first, terminal-native, hypothesis-testing for zero-user products.**

## Signals about who hurts most

Solo technical founders with 0-to-1 products (no users yet) express the most acute pain.
Not growth engineers. Not teams. The solo founder who shipped something good and heard
nothing — that's the person most desperate for a different approach.

Second signal: marketers already using Claude Code informally. They're improvising;
mstack gives them a standard.
