# Hypothesis A: The Crickets Problem

## The bet
**ICP:** Solo technical founder, pre-revenue, has shipped 1-3 products that got no traction. Not a beginner — they can code well. They just don't know why nobody came.

**Primary pain:** "We ship to the sound of crickets because we conflated building with launching." The problem isn't the product. It's that nobody knows how it helps them.

**Wedge:** GTMstack doesn't generate one polished campaign — it does synthetic user research first, then generates 3 cheap positioning hypotheses to test in parallel. This is the approach that works. It's just never been this easy.

**Hook:** You shipped. Nobody came. That's not a product problem.

**Confidence level:** High
**Evidence from research:** "We ship to the sound of crickets" + "I'm done with having 10 side projects with 0 users" + "If your indie project is stuck, it's probably because no one knows how it helps them" — this pain is acute, widespread, and described in almost identical language across independent sources.
**What would prove this wrong:** Founders using GTMstack already have clear ICPs and product-market fit — they just need copy help. If onboarding data shows most users are post-traction, this ICP is wrong.

## Hero copy

### You shipped. Nobody came.
#### GTMstack researches who actually needs your product, then writes 3 different campaigns to find out which one lands.

**Research before copy**
Before writing a word, GTMstack scrapes Reddit, HN, and competitor reviews to find how real people describe your problem space. Real language beats founder language every time.

**3 hypotheses, not 1 campaign**
You don't know yet which ICP hurts most. GTMstack generates 3 campaigns with different audiences, pains, and hooks — designed to test cheap in parallel before you spend real money.

**Assumption tracking**
Every campaign auto-generates a list of what it's betting on. Run `/mretro` after two weeks to see which assumptions held.
